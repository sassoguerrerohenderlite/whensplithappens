Our Story

When Split Happens was created to bring clarity to one of life’s most challenging transitions—divorce. Produced by Sasso Guerrero & Henderlite, a leading family law firm in Jacksonville, Florida, this podcast was designed to help individuals and families better understand the emotional, financial, and legal realities of separation. Too often, people begin the divorce process uncertain about their rights or overwhelmed by conflicting information. When Split Happens cuts through the confusion with straight talk, real stories, and professional insight from an attorney who has guided hundreds of families through the same process. Each episode reflects our firm’s mission: to empower, educate, and support people navigating family law in Northern Florida.

About the Podcast

When Split Happens is your go-to podcast for navigating the emotional, legal, and financial complexities of divorce and family law. Hosted by Jay Henderlite, a Board Certified Family Law Attorney at Sasso Guerrero & Henderlite, the podcast explores key issues like child custody, alimony, property division, co-parenting, and post-divorce adjustments. Jay breaks down each topic with clear, practical explanations and straightforward advice you can use. Expect candid conversations, myth-busting insights, and guidance that helps you turn uncertainty into informed action. Whether you’re preparing for divorce, managing custody disputes, or seeking to better understand Florida family law, When Split Happens delivers expert clarity with compassion and purpose.

About the Host

Jay Henderlite is a Board Certified Marital and Family Law Specialist and managing partner at Sasso Guerrero & Henderlite, one of Northeast Florida’s most respected family law firms. With decades of experience representing clients in complex divorce and custody matters, Jay brings both deep legal knowledge and real-world understanding to every conversation. Known for his straightforward approach and calm professionalism, Jay helps listeners navigate difficult topics with confidence and clarity. His goal is to simplify the family law process and provide practical, actionable guidance to those facing divorce, custody challenges, or co-parenting struggles.

When he’s not in the courtroom or the recording studio, Jay is passionate about educating the public on family law issues and empowering people to make informed decisions about their futures.

Who Should Listen

When Split Happens is designed for anyone dealing with the realities of divorce, custody, or co-parenting. Whether you’re just beginning to explore your legal options or already deep in the process, this podcast offers the tools, knowledge, and perspective you need to move forward. Listeners include parents navigating custody and time-sharing, spouses managing property division and financial planning, and individuals seeking to understand their rights under Florida family law. It’s also valuable for friends and family who want to support loved ones through separation.

By blending expert legal guidance with empathy and honesty, When Split Happens provides more than just legal education—it helps listeners rebuild stability, protect their families, and find hope after separation.

About Sasso Guerrero & Henderlite

Sasso Guerrero & Henderlite is a premier family law firm based in Jacksonville, Florida, with offices throughout Northeast Florida including Downtown Jacksonville, Southside, St. Johns, Beaches, and Fernandina Beach. The firm is led by three Board Certified Marital & Family Law Experts, making it the only firm in the region with this distinction.

At SGH, we don’t just practice family law—we live it. We are parents and advocates who understand the personal and emotional weight behind every case. Our attorneys handle all aspects of family law, including divorce, child custody, adoption, assisted reproductive technology (ART) law, and LGBTQ+ family matters.

Our mission is simple: to deliver exceptional advocacy with compassion and professionalism. We guide clients through complex legal processes, helping them make informed decisions that protect what matters most—their families and their futures.
